# Research on "Iruma Folk Jamboree" (Test Investigation)

This report compiles findings on the "Iruma Folk Jamboree" (formally "Irumagawa Folk Jamboree") based on the premise defined in the context.

## Overview (概要)
*   **Claim**: The "Irumagawa Folk Jamboree" was a grassroots outdoor music event held in Iruma City, Saitama Prefecture, from 1994 to 2008. It was initiated by Shoichi Matsunaga (known as "Matsu-sho").
*   **Evidence**: The event started in February 1994 at the outdoor stage of "Atarashiki wo Shiru Park" (Know the New Park). It was held monthly (later bimonthly) and concluded with its 100th session in August 2008.
*   **Source**: [community_data/tier3] [Ayumi of Iruma Sound Heaven](https://rosalandrecord.jimdofree.com/profile-%E3%83%97%E3%83%AD%E3%83%95%E3%82%A3%E3%83%BC%E3%83%AB/%E5%85%A5%E9%96%93%E3%82%B5%E3%82%A6%E3%83%B3%E3%83%89%E3%83%98%E3%83%B4%E3%83%B3%E3%81%AE%E3%81%82%E3%82%86%E3%81%BF/) (2024-03-15) — primary_source: true (Organizer's record)
*   **Confidence**: high

## Protagonist: Shoichi Matsunaga (発起人：松しょう氏)
*   **Claim**: Shoichi Matsunaga (1953-), a musician and former leader of the 1970s folk group "Gam Gam", launched the event to create a space for handmade music culture.
*   **Evidence**: Matsunaga debuted in 1970 with the duo "Akaten" and later led "Gam Gam" (Express/Toshiba EMI). After the group disbanded in 1976, he worked at Shinko Music and later opened "Live Cafe Matsu" in Sayama. He launched the Jamboree in 1994 as a personal project which grew into a community movement.
*   **Source**: [community_data/tier3] [Gam Gam - Wikipedia](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%81%8C%E3%82%80%E3%81%8C%E3%82%80) (2024-03-15)
*   **Confidence**: high

## Historical Context & Location (社会的文脈と地域性)
*   **Claim**: While the event started in 1994, its spiritual roots lie in the 1970s "All Japan Folk Jamboree" and the unique "Hyde Park" culture of the Iruma/Sayama area.
*   **Evidence**:
    *   **70s Legacy**: The Iruma/Sayama area (specifically the former Johnson Air Base housing, or "American Village") was a hub for musicians like Haruomi Hosono and the band Happy End in the 1970s, fostering a unique blend of American folk/rock and Japanese sensibilities.
    *   **Event Philosophy**: Matsunaga inherited the 1960s-70s folk spirit of "singing in one's own words" and "anti-commercialism." The event was run entirely by volunteers (DIY spirit), devoid of major production companies, echoing the ethos of the original Nakatsugawa Folk Jamboree.
*   **Source**: [academic_paper/tier2] [A Study on US Military Base Culture in the 1970s: Focusing on "Sayama American Village"](https://koara.lib.keio.ac.jp/xoonips/modules/xoonips/download.php?koara_id=AA11358103-20170701-0099) (2024-03-15)
*   **Confidence**: medium (Direct link between Matsu-sho's motivation and Hyde Park culture is inferred from regional context)

## Chronology (沿革と終焉)
*   **Claim**: The event evolved from a solo stage to an organized committee system, concluding at its 100th session due to the organizer's decision to "close the chapter."
*   **Evidence**:
    *   **1994**: Started as a solo performance by Matsu-sho.
    *   **1998 (50th)**: Shifted to bimonthly schedule.
    *   **2008 (100th)**: Concluded. Matsunaga decided to end it to mark a clear milestone rather than letting it fade away.
    *   **Post-2008**: Succeeded by "Iruma Sound Heaven," which continues the legacy in a different format.
*   **Source**: [community_data/tier3] [Official Blog "Irublo"](https://blog.goo.ne.jp/iruten/e/53c677a532e17b492ea0dde53c0e7de4) (2024-03-15)
*   **Confidence**: high

## Conclusion (結論)
The "Iruma Folk Jamboree" (1994-2008) was a revival of the 1970s folk spirit, orchestrated by 70s folk veteran Shoichi Matsunaga in a region (Iruma/Sayama) deeply connected to the origins of Japanese folk-rock. It prioritized community and DIY ethics over commercial success, serving as a bridge between the 70s movements and modern local music festivals.
